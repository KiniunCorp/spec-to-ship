# Sponsor Recognition Policy

This document defines how supporters of Spec-To-Ship are recognized.

The goal is to:

- acknowledge support transparently
- avoid over-commercialization
- maintain trust in the open-source nature of the project

## Principles

- Recognition should be clear but not intrusive
- Sponsorship should never compromise project direction
- All users should receive the same core product experience

## Individual supporters

Individual supporters may be recognized through:

- inclusion in a public supporters list (when enabled)
- occasional acknowledgements in release notes or updates

No functional benefits are tied to individual sponsorship.

## Company sponsors

Company sponsors may be recognized through:

- name and logo placement (when applicable) in a sponsor section
- mention in project updates or announcements
- optional inclusion in a public sponsor page

Higher-tier sponsors may be invited to:

- periodic roadmap discussions
- feedback sessions

## What sponsors do NOT receive

Sponsorship does not provide:

- guaranteed feature delivery
- priority merging of pull requests
- exclusive access to features
- control over the roadmap

## Separation from services

Any paid services (consulting, workshops, etc.) are separate from sponsorship.

Recognition applies to sponsorship only, not to service engagements.

## Changes to this policy

This policy may evolve as the project grows.

Any changes will be reflected in this document.
